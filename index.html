( about the website )

Ming Dynasty (1368–1644): This motif was commonly seen during the Xuande (宣德, 1426–1435) and Jiajing (嘉靖, 1522–1566) periods, where artisans used deep cobalt blue pigments to create intricate wave patterns that symbolized imperial stability and maritime strength. Qing Dynasty (1644–1912): The design reached its peak refinement during the Kangxi (康熙, 1662–1722), Yongzheng (雍正, 1723–1735), and Qianlong (乾隆, 1736–1796) periods, with more elaborate detailing and often paired with dragons (龙) and phoenixes (凤) as symbols of power and prosperity.
